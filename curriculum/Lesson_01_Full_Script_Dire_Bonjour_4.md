### Core Lesson · Unit 1 · Full production voice script — Revision 3

**New words:** 12 · **A1 progress after this lesson:** 12 / 235

---

## How to read this script

Four voices, alternated by gender at section boundaries (unchanged): **NARRATOR (EN · MAN/WOMAN)** for the host, **FRENCH VOICE (FR · MAN/WOMAN)** for the model voice. French and English are never the same gender at the same moment; the pair flips only at the 🎙️ section banners.

**What changed in this revision:**

- **The French voice now says only taught vocabulary — nothing else, ever.** No "Voilà," "Exactement," "Bravo, tu as fait..." or any other untaught French, including small connector words. A day-one learner can't tell your curriculum from your filler, so if it isn't one of the 12 words or a sentence built from them, it doesn't get said. The one exception is a bare "Bravo !" at the very end — close enough to an English loanword that it doesn't introduce anything to decode.
- **Every word now gets a fixed three-pass listen-and-repeat**, prompted once by the narrator: normal speed → slower/clearer → normal speed again, with a short pause after each pass for the learner to echo that specific pass. No spoken confirmation afterward — the next beat simply starts.
- **Two pronunciation call-outs added** (on bonjour's nasal vowel and au revoir's French r) — the kind of specific listen-for-this-sound note that's worth the extra seconds. Not on every word; that would be the same wordiness problem in a new costume.
- **Phase badges added throughout.** Every time the lesson moves into Meet, Echo, Build, Make It Yours, Recall, or Recap, a big-font title card announces it and a small corner badge holds that label until it changes. This replaces spoken transitions with a visual one, so the narrator doesn't have to say "now let's..." every time.

Markup key: *[bracketed italics]* = Eleven v3 audio tag. Ellipses inside a line = micro-pause within one clip. ⏸ PAUSE — Ns = silent gap in the Remotion timeline, not sent to ElevenLabs. 🖼 = on-screen visual/text/phase cue.

### Production notes

- The "slower" pass uses the `[slowly]` audio tag. If your voice doesn't slow down enough from the tag alone, reinforce it in the text itself with a syllable break (e.g. `Bon... jour`) — v3 responds to text structure as well as tags.
- Eleven v3 (GA since Feb 2026) doesn't support SSML breaks — pauses stay in the Remotion timeline, as before.
- Generate each quoted line as its own API call.
